  • PROBLEM
  • There is currently no efficient way to onboard or modify existing programs across multiple hotels simultaneously. Hilton’s internal GDM team is required to update each hotel individually, resulting in a time-consuming and resource-intensive process.
  • GOAL
  • Design a streamlined approach that allows users to manage and execute large-scale updates across numerous hotel properties.
IMPACT & OUTCOME

Before the Bulk service was created in Prodigy, the GDM (Global Distribution Management)team would manage a Hotel one at a time. Hilton has 23 brands, making up aroung 7,500 hotels. When new add-on(s) become active or old ones need updating, it is a hefy task for the GDM team to onboard.

In 2024, delivered efficiencies for team members onboarding new hotels, saved 1600 hours of work.

Increased the capabilities and number of hotels managing day-to-day work from 1,950 to 4,000.

ABOUT BULK & CHALLENGES

The Bulk Service was developed in response to a critical business need: enabling large-scale updates across hundreds, or even thousands, of hotels at once. Add-ons is the first space that will enable the Bulk Service integration. As part of the bulk serivce process, an approval process is required.

One of Hilton’s biggest challenges is the lack of standardization across its 23 brands and global regions. As a result, the Bulk Service cannot rely on a universal standard or context when validating whether the input data is appropriate for a given set of hotels, making the validation process significantly more complex.
