Room Attributes

An experience designed to better define the unique qualities that make each room distinctive.

  • DELIVERABLE
  • Requirements Gathering, Sketching, Prototype & Design Comps
  • ROLE
  • Lead UX Designer
  • TOOLS & SOFTWARE
  • Figma, Axure, Usertesting.com, UCD Research Methodologies, Jira & Confluence
  • PROBLEM
  • Currently, Hilton uses long-form descriptions to communicate the unique attributes of each room type. However, this approach makes it difficult for potential guests to clearly understand what’s included in their purchase—especially given character limitations and the lack of searchability or filter options in the browse and booking experience.
  • GOAL
  • Establish a centralized process for Hilton team members and hotel staff to manage room attributes efficiently.
OUTCOME

Room Attributes went live in Prodigy on June 1, 2025. The intial POC at 50 hotels has seen a 9% increase in bookings,reduction of time on page by 33%, and reduction of user session by 28% . Now that room attributes can be defined more clearely at the room type level, users can effceintly search and filter down to thier need more easy. In turn, this means the user can better understand the cost of the room and the hotel better define the value and price of each room.

HILTON TOOL ANALYSIS & USER RESEARCH

HILTON TOOL ANALYSIS

My first task was to track down where and how attributes are managaed and maintained in the Hilton ecosystem.

  • RESEARCH TAKEAWAYS
  • Customers have to sift through large amounts of text to compare room types.
    The key value differences between room types are primarily conveyed through unstructured description content.
    The unstructured descriptions are often lengthy and get truncated in many areas, making it difficult for customers to compare room options effectively.
    Users currently lack the ability to filter or search by specific room attributes.
    Managing 65 room types pushes the ONQ R&I system to its operational limits.
SOLUTION

HIGH FIEDELITY DESIGNS

SOLUTION

Within the Rooms product, there are two areas where users can assign or manage attributes. Due to permission settings, hotel users are limited to assigning attributes only, while internal team members have access to both assigning and managing attributes.

Manage Attributes - is a space to create, update and delete attributes and attribute categories. For instance, Bay View is a single attribute that belongs to the View category.

Assign Attributes - is a feature that allows users to associate specific attributes with a hotel’s room types. Room types are a generalized structure used to group similar rooms. In the future, Hilton aims to move toward defining rooms individually by room number, rather than relying on grouped room types
